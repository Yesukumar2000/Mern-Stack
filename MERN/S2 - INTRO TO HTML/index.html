GUNTUR

Guntur is known for its chilli, cotton and tobacco exports and has the largest chilli market yard in Asia.The city region is surrounded by many ancient temples and sites that include Kondavidu, Amararama, Caves, Pedakakani.The district is known for being the top producer and exporter of chilies and chili powder from India. It also exports cotton and tobacco

Top 5 places in Guntur

1.Mirchi Yard

1.Guntur chillies are a group of chilli cultivars from the Guntur and Prakasam districts of Andhra Pradesh, India.They are renowned globally and exported to Asia, Canada, and Europe.The main trading place for the Guntur chilli is called Guntur Mirchi Yard, which is Asia's largest dried red chilli market.Market prices for the chillies are accessible on the National Agriculture Market.

2.Undavalli Caves

2.Set near the Krishna River, Undavalli is a tranquil farmland and residential area known for being home to the imposing 4-level, carved-stone Undavalli Caves, which date from the 4th century. The district features many churches, colourful temples, businesses, and stores. Ice cream shops and straightforward restaurants are primarily found along Undavalli

3.Kondaveedu

3.Kondaveedu Fort is a historically significant ancient hill fortress located in Kondaveedu, a village in the Chilakaluripet constituency of Palnadu district, Andhra Pradesh, India. The site is located 16 miles west of the city of Guntur. Apart from this main fort, there are two other forts nearby.

4.Amaravati

4.Amaravati is the capital of the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh. It is located on the right bank of the river Krishna in Guntur district. Dharanikota, the ancient city site nearby, was founded more than 2,200 years ago, serving as an ancient capital.

5.Suryalanka Beach

5.Suryalanka Beach, also known as Bapatla Beach, is famous for its natural beauty, wide and spacious waters, and clear views. It's located in the Bapatla district of Andhra Pradesh, about 9 kilometers from Bapatla

6.Kotappakonda

6.Originally known as Kondakavuru, this place is famously known as Kotappakonda or Trikutaparvattam meaning three-peaked hill which actually lies nearby. Located nearly 25 miles from the city of Guntur, it offers a panoramic view of majestic hills from any direction.
